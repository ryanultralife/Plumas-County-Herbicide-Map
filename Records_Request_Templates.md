# Records request templates — SprayMap California

Copy-paste templates for `spraymapca@gmail.com`. Replace anything in [brackets].
Keep the tone neutral and factual — you'll get faster, fuller cooperation than with
adversarial language. Send from the org Gmail and keep a copy of every request and
response (this is also what feeds the public Transparency ledger later).

Two legal tracks, depending on who holds the records:
- **State/county records → California Public Records Act (CPRA)**, Gov. Code § 7920.000 et seq.
  Agencies must issue a *determination* within **10 calendar days** (Gov. Code § 7922.535;
  +14 days in unusual circumstances). Pesticide-use data, permits, and Notices of Intent
  are held by the **California Dept. of Pesticide Regulation (DPR)** and the **County
  Agricultural Commissioner** in each county.
- **Federal records → Freedom of Information Act (FOIA)**, 5 U.S.C. § 552. Agencies must
  respond within **20 business days**. For national-forest spraying (FACTS data, NEPA
  project files), that's the **USDA Forest Service**. Submit at **foia.gov** or the USDA
  Public Access Link (PAL); request a public-interest fee waiver.

## 1. CPRA request — California Dept. of Pesticide Regulation (DPR)

**Submit at:** DPR's records portal https://cdprca.nextrequest.com (preferred), or email publicrecords@cdpr.ca.gov
**Subject:** California Public Records Act request — Pesticide Use Report data, [counties / statewide], [year range]

> Note: DPR also publishes annual Pesticide Use Report data in bulk for download — check
> the DPR PUR data page first; you may not need a formal request for the historical sets.

Dear Public Records Coordinator,

Under the California Public Records Act (Gov. Code § 7920.000 et seq.), SprayMap
California requests copies of the following public records:

1. Pesticide Use Report (PUR) records for [all California counties / the following
   counties: ____], for the period [Jan 1, 20__ through Dec 31, 20__], including for
   each application: date, county, location identifier (PLSS section/township/range or
   site ID), product/registration number, pounds and/or gallons applied, acres treated,
   application method, crop/site, operator/permittee, and applicator.
2. Any data dictionary or record layout needed to interpret the above.

Please provide the records in an electronic, machine-readable format (CSV, Excel, or
database export) sent to this email address. If the full PUR dataset is already published
in bulk, a link or copy of the most recent release for the period above is acceptable.

We request a waiver of fees. SprayMap California is a non-commercial public-interest
project; the records will be used to inform the public about pesticide use and water
quality, and the results will be published free of charge. If any fees apply, please
notify us before incurring costs exceeding $25.

Please confirm receipt and provide your determination within 10 days as required by
Gov. Code § 7922.535. We're happy to narrow or clarify scope to speed processing.

Thank you,
[Your name], SprayMap California

## 3. FOIA request — USDA Forest Service

**Submit at:** https://www.foia.gov (select USDA → Forest Service) or USDA PAL.
**Subject:** FOIA request — herbicide/pesticide treatment records, [Forest name or "all California national forests"], [year range]

To the Forest Service FOIA Officer,

Under the Freedom of Information Act, 5 U.S.C. § 552, SprayMap California requests the
following records for [the ___ National Forest / all national forests in California] for
[fiscal years 20__–20__]:

1. FACTS (Forest Service Activity Tracking System) records for chemical/herbicide
   treatment activities, including activity type, NEPA project, ranger district, acres
   accomplished and planned, treatment date, status (completed/planned), and coordinates.
2. The active ingredients and product names, application rates, and total quantities
   applied for those treatments.
3. The associated NEPA decision documents (EA/EIS, Decision Notices/Records of Decision)
   authorizing the treatments.

Please provide records electronically in machine-readable format where available
(CSV/Excel/shapefile/GeoJSON) and PDFs for the decision documents.

**Fee waiver:** We request a waiver of all fees. Disclosure is in the public interest
because it will contribute significantly to public understanding of government pesticide
use on public lands, and it is not in any commercial interest of the requester. SprayMap
California is a non-commercial public-interest project that publishes its findings free of
charge; please classify this request accordingly. If a waiver is denied, please notify us
before incurring fees over $25.

Please confirm receipt and your tracking number. We're glad to narrow scope (e.g., by
forest or year) to assist processing within the 20-business-day period.

Thank you,

### Tips
- Track every request: date sent, agency, what you asked, and the response/tracking
  number. A simple sheet works; it also documents your work for the Transparency ledger.
- If an agency misses the deadline, a brief polite follow-up citing the statute
  (§ 7922.535 for CPRA; the 20-day rule for FOIA) usually moves things.
- Narrow scope (specific counties/forests/years) gets faster, cheaper results than
  "everything statewide" — consider phasing requests.
- For CPRA, you do **not** have to explain why you want records, but stating the
  public-interest purpose helps your fee-waiver request.
